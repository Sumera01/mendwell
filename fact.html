Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is mental health? +

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act, and also how we handle stress, relate to others, and make decisions.

How do I know if I have a mental health disorder? +

If you experience persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, or irritability, or have trouble functioning in daily life, it could indicate a mental health disorder. Seeking help from a professional is important.

What should I do if I feel anxious or depressed? +

It's essential to talk to someone you trust or consult a mental health professional. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and other therapies can be very helpful in managing anxiety and depression.

Is mental health treatment expensive? +

The cost of treatment can vary. Many professionals offer sliding scale fees based on your income. Additionally, some insurance plans cover mental health services.

How can I improve my mental health? +

Improving mental health involves maintaining a balanced lifestyle, including physical activity, a healthy diet, good sleep hygiene, and strong social connections. Seeking professional help when needed is also essential.

What are the symptoms of anxiety? +

Common symptoms of anxiety include constant worry, restlessness, fatigue, difficulty concentrating, and physical symptoms like sweating and rapid heartbeat.

What is Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)? +

CBT is a form of psychotherapy that helps people identify and change negative thought patterns and behaviors that contribute to their mental health issues, including depression and anxiety.

What is the difference between stress and anxiety? +

Stress is a response to a specific external situation, while anxiety is a persistent feeling of worry or fear that can occur even without an identifiable cause.

Can mental health disorders be treated without medication? +

Yes, many mental health disorders can be treated with therapy, lifestyle changes, and social support, though medication may also be recommended for some individuals depending on the severity of their symptoms.
