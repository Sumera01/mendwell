What is Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)?

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with daily functioning or development. People with ADHD may find it difficult to focus, follow through with tasks, or regulate their impulses.

ADHD can affect individuals at all ages, although it is often diagnosed in childhood. With appropriate treatment, people with ADHD can learn strategies to manage their symptoms effectively.

Symptoms of Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder

The symptoms of ADHD can vary, but common signs include:

  • Inattention: Difficulty focusing on tasks, forgetfulness, and losing things frequently.
  • Hyperactivity: Excessive talking, fidgeting, and difficulty staying seated in situations where it is expected.
  • Impulsivity: Difficulty waiting for turns, interrupting others, and making decisions without thinking about the consequences.
  • Difficulty Organizing Tasks: Struggling to prioritize tasks and meet deadlines.

Causes of Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder

The exact cause of ADHD is unknown, but several factors may contribute to its development:

  • Genetics: ADHD often runs in families, indicating a genetic predisposition to the disorder.
  • Brain Structure and Function: Certain brain structures and functions, especially related to attention and self-regulation, may be affected in individuals with ADHD.
  • Environmental Factors: Exposure to toxins, premature birth, or prenatal factors may increase the risk of developing ADHD.

Treatment for Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder

There are several effective treatments available to help manage ADHD symptoms:

  • Behavioral Therapy: Behavioral interventions help individuals with ADHD develop coping strategies and improve organizational skills.
  • Medication: Stimulant medications, such as methylphenidate and amphetamines, are commonly prescribed to help manage ADHD symptoms by increasing dopamine levels in the brain.
  • Educational Support: Classroom accommodations and structured environments can help individuals with ADHD succeed in school.

Consulting with a healthcare provider can help determine the best treatment plan for each individual.
